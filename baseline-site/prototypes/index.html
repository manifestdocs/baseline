Baseline Language

Homepage Design Concepts

Nine distinct visual approaches for the Baseline programming language homepage. Each is a complete, standalone design with a distinct visual atmosphere and thematic direction.

The type is the spec. Effects are data. Designed for the era of machine-generated code.

Production Homepage

The production homepage for baseline-lang.org. Based on Dark Bringhurst with sticky nav, outcome-first structure, hero code block, heritage section, audience cards, and compiler experience showcase.

The Monolith

Brutalist structures, enormous typography, warm amber atmospheric glow. Imposing architecture, engineered precision, otherworldly scale.
Design 02

The Baseline Test

Terminal interrogation aesthetic. Call-and-response rhythm. Cold blue-white text on black. Scan lines. Every question answered by code.
Design 03

The Hologram

Translucent, ethereal, layered. Glass-like containers with subtle refractions. Digital becoming tangible. Light taking shape.
Design 04

The Sea Wall

Vast panoramic layout. Warm golden light. Features like solar panels -- grid-based, modular, repeating. Standing at the edge of something vast.
Design 05

The Memory

Intimate vertical storytelling. Warm wood tones and cool tech. Organic typography, natural textures. Code, like memory, builds meaning layer by layer.
Design 06

The Systems Pitch

Technical sales pitch. Why Baseline fixes what other languages got wrong -- for connected systems, concurrency, HTTP-native APIs, and the agentic age of AI-assisted development.
Design 07

The Tufte

Information-dense long-form design inspired by Edward Tufte. Sidenotes, margin figures, minimal decoration. The content is the interface -- every pixel conveys information.
Design 08

The Bringhurst

Cream paper, deep red title, Georgia serif, fleuron dividers. Edward Tufte information design on Robert Bringhurst's color palette. Typography exists to honor content.

The Dark Bringhurst

Near-black, warm copper accents, cream text. The version 3.0 cover palette -- Tufte information design in Bringhurst's own colors. Typographic elegance in darkness.
